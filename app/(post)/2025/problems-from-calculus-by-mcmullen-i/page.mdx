# On the Beauty of Mathematical Notation

Mathematics is not merely a languageâ€”it is a symphony of symbols, a choreography of logic and form. To test the robustness of our `Math` component, we present a flurry of expressions both elementary and esoteric.

Consider the **Pythagorean Theorem** in its most basic form:  
<Math type="inline">a^2 + b^2 = c^2</Math>

## Triple Integrals and Volume

A fundamental result in multivariable calculus is the computation of volume via triple integrals. For a region <Math type="inline">E</Math> in 3D space, the volume is given by:

<Math type="block" tex="\iiint_E f(x, y, z) \, dx\, dy\, dz" />

In spherical coordinates, where <Math type="inline">x = \rho\sin\phi\cos\theta</Math>, <Math type="inline">y = \rho\sin\phi\sin\theta</Math>, and <Math type="inline">z = \rho\cos\phi</Math>, the Jacobian determinant introduces an extra factor of <Math type="inline">\rho^2\sin\phi</Math>. Thus:

<Math type="block" tex="\iiint_E f(\rho, \phi, \theta) \rho^2 \sin\phi \, d\rho\, d\phi\, d\theta" />

## Summation and Limits

The Basel problem, solved by Euler, explores the value of the infinite sum of reciprocals of squares:

<Math type="block" tex="\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} = \frac{\pi^2}{6}" />

Nested summations can test vertical alignment and large limits:

<Math type="block" tex="\sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{j=1}^m a_{ij}b_{ji} = \operatorname{tr}(AB)" />

Or even:

<Math type="block" tex="\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^{k+1}}{k} = \ln(2)" />

## Stirling's Approximation and Limits

The behavior of factorials at infinity:

<Math type="block" tex="\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{n!}{\sqrt{2\pi n} \left(\frac{n}{e}\right)^n} = 1" />

## Fourier Transform

A classic in signal analysis:

<Math type="block" tex="\hat{f}(\xi) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x) e^{-2\pi i x \xi} \, dx" />

And the inverse:

<Math type="block" tex="f(x) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \hat{f}(\xi) e^{2\pi i x \xi} \, d\xi" />

## Dirac Delta Function

In distribution theory:

<Math type="block" tex="\int_{-\infty}^\infty f(x)\delta(x-a)\,dx = f(a)" />

## Gaussian Integral

A gem of analysis:

<Math type="block" tex="\int_0^\infty e^{-x^2}\,dx = \tfrac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2}" />
